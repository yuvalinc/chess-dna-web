About Chess DNA

Understand. Improve. Dominate. Chess DNA turns every chess game into progress. Import your Chess.com or Lichess games and get AI insights that find your patterns, fix your weaknesses, and replay every mistake step-by-step.

Find Patterns. Fix Weaknesses. AI detects your worst patterns and helps you break them.

Chess DNA reads every move in every game you import. The AI groups recurring mistakes into named patterns — the kind a coach would point out if they were watching you live.

Example patterns: Missed Tactic, Missed Pin, Missed Fork, Missed Skewer, Missed Mate (in 1–5), Trapped Piece, Discovered Attack, Hanging Pieces, Back Rank Weakness, King Safety, Endgame Technique, Opening Inaccuracy, Pawn Structure, Time Pressure Blunder — and more.

Each pattern shows you how often it happened and how much rating it has cost you. You always know which one to fix first.

Replay. Learn. Get Better. Replay mistakes step-by-step and turn them into strength.

Chess DNA pulls every position where you went wrong and lets you play them again. You see the position, decide on a move, and Chess DNA tells you whether it works. Hints, skips, and the engine's recommendation are one tap away.

You work through your worst pattern first — then the next one. By the time you've cleared a pattern, the mistake doesn't show up in new games.

Instant Game Analysis Clear explanations, key moments, and patterns you can act on.

Every game gets an explanation in plain English — not just numbers. Chess DNA shows you what your move did, why the engine prefers something else, and which pattern this mistake fits into.

Key moments, stats, and pattern tags are all in one view, so a 5-minute game review actually teaches you something.

Your 8-Dimension Skill Radar Track every part of your game and build a complete profile.

Chess DNA scores you across eight skill dimensions:

  • Openings — early-game knowledge and transitions.
  • Tactics — combinations, threats, blunder avoidance.
  • Defense — handling pressure and finding resources.
  • Positional — planning, coordination, pawn structure.
  • Endgame — technique in simplified positions.
  • Calculation — depth and accuracy when it counts.
  • Time Management — clock discipline across phases.
  • Resilience — bouncing back from worse positions.

Your scores roll up to an overall ELO, a percentile, and a rank tier from Pawn to King. Share your Chess DNA card or compare with friends.
